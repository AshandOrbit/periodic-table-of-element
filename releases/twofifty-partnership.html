Element United Partners with TWOFIFTY to Bring Blockchain Verification to Custom and Luxury Jewelry

Luxury jewelry house accepts ELMT tokens, launches digital certificates of authenticity through NextHeir platform

FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE
Salt Lake City, Utah – November 2025

Element United today announced its partnership with TWOFIFTY, a visionary jewelry atelier now accepting ELMT tokens and launching Heirloom Digital Certificates of Authenticity through Element United's NextHeir™ platform.

Every TWOFIFTY piece—from bespoke engagement rings to couture statement pieces—now carries a digital twin through NextHeir, verifying origin, rarity, and carbon impact from mine to masterpiece.

"Great jewelry has always told a story," said Scott Lomu, President of Element United. "Now that story is preserved forever—not in a safe deposit box, but in the fabric of the blockchain itself. TWOFIFTY understands that true luxury demands transparency as exquisite as the piece itself."
"When I design for a woman, I'm crafting her legacy," said Sarah Wilson, co-founder and creative force behind TWOFIFTY. "Element United protects that legacy with the same devotion I pour into every setting, every stone, every detail."

TWOFIFTY's partnership positions Element United to serve the custom, handcrafted, and prestige luxury goods sector at scale—with infrastructure designed for high-volume authentication and global brand integration across exclusive markets.

About TWOFIFTY

TWOFIFTY creates artistic custom jewelry designed for and inspired by remarkable women. Using intentional details and seamless architecture, they specialize in original heirloom pieces with unforgettable runway presence. TWOFIFTY is an art movement celebrating women.

About Element United

Element United provides blockchain-based verification systems spanning natural resource management, mining operations, and asset preservation. Through platforms like Identify.earth, STRIA™, CORE™, and NextHeir, Element United creates transparent, verifiable records that bridge industries—from ethical extraction to family heirlooms, from mine to market to memory.
