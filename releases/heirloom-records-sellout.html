Element United's First Heirloom Digital Records Release Sells Out in 7 Days; Company Announces Expanded Access

Blockchain verification platform sees strong demand for asset preservation system

FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE
Salt Lake City, Utah – November 2025

Element United today announced expanded access to its Heirloom Digital Records Platform after selling out its inaugural release in seven days.

Following strong demand for the first 500 spots, Element United is now opening 1,000 additional spaces for customers seeking permanent, blockchain-backed records for valuable assets, including family heirlooms, art, jewelry, and legal documents.

Families, collectors, and institutions will be able to create verified digital records that serve as proof of authenticity, ownership, and provenance. Upload videos, audio, images, and stories. Element United provides storage, templates, and support to preserve memories permanently.

With verified provenance, secure storage, and blockchain-backed ownership transfer, these permanent records can be shared safely with family members, attorneys, jewelers, and insurance providers.

"We started with ethical gold," said Scott Lomu, President of Element United. "Now we're proving that integrity doesn't stop at the mine—it lives on through every object, every story, every generation."

1,000 spots. Limited time. Reserve at ElementUnited.earth or contact Element United for institutional partnerships.
